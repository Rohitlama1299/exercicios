Faunas of Portugal

Portugal has a diverse fauna due to its varied habitats, which range from the coastal regions to mountainous areas. The country is home to approximately 93 species of mammals, 345 species of birds, 31 species of reptiles, 16 species of amphibians, and a variety of fish and invertebrates.

One of the most iconic mammals in Portugal is the Iberian lynx (Lynx pardinus), which is an endangered species and found mainly in the south of the country. Other mammals found in Portugal include the wild boar (Sus scrofa), the European badger (Meles meles), and the roe deer (Capreolus capreolus)..

Portugal is also an important breeding ground for seabirds, with species such as the common tern (Sterna hirundo), the northern gannet (Morus bassanus), and the European storm petrel (Hydrobates pelagicus) nesting on the country's cliffs and islands.

Overall, Portugal's fauna is an important component of its biodiversity, and its protection is vital for maintaining a healthy and balanced ecosystem. Portugal's fauna provides exciting opportunities for wildlife enthusiasts and researchers to explore and discover the natural world.
